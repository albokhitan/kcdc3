Make a Camera Obscura

Instructor

Yonas Hassen is a native Washingtonian and avid film photographer with a collection of far too many cameras. Although Yonas is averse to complex debates over Ilford vs. Kodak, he does enjoy a good geek-out session about the craft and dying art of film photography. He has been a photographer for nearly 17 years.

ato-yonas.com

@yonas

Make your own camera obscura out of (mostly) recycled materials. We’ll light-proof a cardboard box and add a pinhole to act as a lens. Light will stream through the pinhole and create an upside-down image of the scene outside the box. Camera obscuras have been tourist attractions, vehicles for experiments with light, and even tools used by great masters to create their paintings. 

Bring your own cardboard box, one big enough to fit over your head with extra room. Something around 16 in. wide and 2–3 feet tall would work. You won’t need any photography or craft skills, but you don’t want to be afraid of the dark!
